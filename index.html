LumaFlow Gii
Desktop photo uploader for Google Drive & Google Photos

About this app

LumaFlow Gii is a desktop application for macOS that helps photographers send photos from cameras or folders directly to Google Drive and Google Photos. It is installed locally on the user’s computer and does not provide a public web interface to other users.

Platform
macOS (Intel & Apple Silicon)
Type
Native desktop app (not SaaS)
Developer
Kru-Gap / GAP-FOTO

How Google data is used

LumaFlow Gii uses Google OAuth to let each user sign in with their own Google Account. The app uses the following permissions (OAuth scopes):

  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.file – upload and manage files created by this app in the user’s Google Drive.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/photoslibrary.appendonly – add photos to the user’s Google Photos library and albums.
  • https://www.googleapis.com/auth/drive.photos.readonly – (optional) read existing photos when the user chooses related features.

All access is performed only on behalf of the signed-in user. Tokens are stored locally on the user’s machine and are not shared with any external servers controlled by the developer.

Downloads

The app is distributed as macOS DMG packages for:

  • Intel (x64)

If you received LumaFlow Gii from the developer, you can install it by opening the DMG and dragging the app into the Applications folder.
